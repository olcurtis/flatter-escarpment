Clean air, fresh water, and abundant natural light are the universal ingredients to sustain life. We charge architects to find forms that balance these within the physical constraints of site, space, and time.

A compact form emerges as the process blends experienced hands with new materials and methods. We combined these with the lessons of history, the research of today, and an awareness of human behavior to build a better structure.

This treemap is a visual representation of construction costs divided into six partitions. Stewart Brand's adaptation at left was based on the earlier work of Frank Duffy. Organizing a heirarchy of costs, quickly reveals there is no silver bullet to reducing the cost of quality housing.

Tip: The best way to find potential savings is to work backwards from the largest expenses. But remember, reducing the structure or the things that protect the structure (i.e. roof, moisture barriers, flashing details) usually isn't the right place to compromise. Have you ever wondered why some houses have cheap windows and vinyl siding? Now, you know.
